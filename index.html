Complex Numbers Are Not Imaginary

A circle of radius π, tilted in 3D. The "imaginary" axis is simply the dimension you stop seeing.

0°
━━ True circle (r = π) ╌╌ What you see (projection) ╌╌ What "i" encodes (hidden depth)

At 0° you see the full circle. As you tilt, one axis compresses — it hasn't vanished, it went orthogonal to your view. That hidden component is exactly what the "imaginary" part of a complex number encodes. Nothing imaginary about it. Gauss wanted to call them lateral numbers. He was right.
